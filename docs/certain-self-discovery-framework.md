# FI-YOU Certain Self-Discovery Framework

Last updated: 2026-06-25

Owner: Product Concept / Insight Engine / UX Writing

## Product Intent

FI-YOU should not feel like an app that says, "It may be something like this."

Clues are the input. The user-facing output should be an analysis result.

FI-YOU does not permanently define the person, but when it presents a result, it
must present that result clearly.

The service should help the user receive clear answers about themselves:

- what they like and dislike
- what they repeatedly care about
- what kinds of work or roles fit their energy, values, and behavior
- what kinds of work or roles drain them
- what kinds of people, teams, and relationship patterns fit them
- what kinds of people or environments create friction
- what personality patterns, tendencies, temperament, motivations, and stress responses show up repeatedly
- what is changing over time

The promise is not supernatural certainty. The promise is record-based certainty:

> "Based on what you have repeatedly shown, FI-YOU can say this clearly."

This means:

- Do not say "this is your fixed identity."
- Do say "this is the result FI-YOU reached from your current records."
- Do not say "this is just a clue" after evidence is strong.
- Do say "these clues now support this analysis result."

## Core Principle

FI-YOU must be careful about overclaiming, but it must not be vague.

Do not solve safety by weakening every sentence. Solve safety by separating:

1. Evidence
2. Confidence level
3. Interpretation domain
4. User control

When evidence is strong, the product should speak directly.

Weak:

- "You seem to maybe prefer quiet environments."
- "This could mean you are interested in autonomy."

Strong:

- "Your records repeatedly point to quiet, self-directed environments as a better fit."
- "Autonomy is one of the clearest values in your current record."
- "Analysis result: you fit better in environments with ownership, depth, and low interruption."

Unsafe overclaim:

- "You are an introvert."
- "You must avoid people-facing jobs."
- "This is your true personality."

## Certainty Levels

All insights should be assigned one of these product-facing certainty levels.

### 1. Observed

Use when there are 1-2 signals.

Meaning:

- FI-YOU has noticed a clue.
- It is not enough to present as a stable pattern.

Tone:

- concrete but light
- no identity claim

Example:

- "Your recent answer showed stronger pull toward independent decision-making than group alignment."

### 2. Repeated

Use when there are 3-7 supporting signals across answers, Diary entries, or U-Map axes.

Meaning:

- FI-YOU can name a pattern clearly.
- The pattern can still change with future records.

Tone:

- direct
- no apologetic language
- avoid "maybe", "seems", "could be" unless explaining limits

Example:

- "Independent decision-making is a repeated pattern in your current record."
- "You consistently choose environments where you can control pace, depth, and standards."

### 3. Strong

Use when there are 8+ signals, repeated across at least two source types or two parent domains.

Meaning:

- FI-YOU can present this as one of the user's clearest current patterns.
- Still not a medical, clinical, hiring, or destiny claim.

Tone:

- confident
- useful
- specific

Example:

- "Autonomy is one of your clearest current values."
- "You are likely to fit work that gives you ownership, quiet focus time, and visible standards of quality."

## Required Output Shape

Every meaningful self-discovery result should answer four things:

1. Clear statement
2. Evidence
3. Fit / friction
4. Next check

Example:

```text
Clear statement:
Autonomy is one of your clearest current values.

Evidence:
It appeared in 9 records across decision style, life direction, and Diary entries.

Fit:
You will likely feel stronger in roles where you own decisions, set standards, and protect deep work time.

Friction:
You may tire faster in environments with constant approval loops, unclear authority, or frequent context switching.

Next check:
The next questions should test whether this autonomy is mainly about freedom, responsibility, privacy, or creative control.
```

The clear statement should be labeled as a result, not as a loose clue.

Use:

- "Analysis result"
- "Current result"
- "Clear result"
- "FI-YOU result"

Avoid making the final output sound unfinished:

- "Small clue"
- "Maybe"
- "This seems to show"
- "It may be worth looking at"

## Domains FI-YOU Should Cover

The current taxonomy already covers many source nodes. Product surfaces should group them into user-legible result domains.

### Preference

Answers:

- likes
- dislikes
- comfortable conditions
- draining conditions
- preferred rhythm
- preferred feedback style
- preferred relationship distance

### Interest

Answers:

- topics the user returns to
- activities that create curiosity
- problems the user naturally notices
- areas where the user seeks depth

### Aptitude Fit

Answers:

- work modes that match the user's energy and decision style
- strengths shown through repeated behavior
- environments where the user can sustain performance
- environments likely to create friction

Important:

- Do not claim professional ability without performance evidence.
- Say "fit" or "working condition fit", not "guaranteed talent".

### Career Type Fit

Answers:

- role families
- work environment families
- collaboration styles
- pace and structure
- degree of ambiguity or autonomy

Safe examples:

- "strategy/research-heavy roles"
- "craft-focused individual contributor roles"
- "people-coordination roles with clear boundaries"
- "early-stage ambiguous environments"
- "stable operational environments"

Avoid:

- "You should become a doctor."
- "This job is your destiny."
- "This career is objectively right for you."

### Relationship Fit

Answers:

- people the user communicates well with
- people who may create friction
- team cultures that fit
- emotional distance and closeness patterns
- conflict and repair style

Important:

- FI-YOU can describe relationship fit patterns.
- FI-YOU should not judge another person or predict relationship success.

Strong safe example:

- "You fit better with people who communicate expectations directly and respect quiet recovery time."

Unsafe:

- "This person is bad for you."
- "You are incompatible."

### Personality / Tendency / Temperament

Answers:

- recurring behavior patterns
- emotional rhythm
- motivation
- stress response
- decision style
- social energy
- self-image

Important:

- Use descriptive pattern language, not fixed type labels.

Strong safe example:

- "Your current record shows a high need for inner standards, careful choice, and private recovery."

Unsafe:

- "You are a perfectionist type."
- "You are avoidant."

## Language Rules

Use direct language when evidence supports it.

Prefer:

- "is a repeated pattern"
- "is one of your clearest current values"
- "fits your current record"
- "creates friction in your current record"
- "shows up across several records"
- "is worth treating as important"
- "is not a one-time signal anymore"

Avoid default vagueness:

- "seems"
- "maybe"
- "appears to"
- "could be"
- "a little"
- "in some ways"

Use limits only where they help trust:

- "current record"
- "based on the records so far"
- "not a fixed identity"
- "future records can change this"

Do not use limits to weaken the core answer.

Weak:

- "This is not certain, but maybe autonomy is important."

Better:

- "Autonomy is clear in your current record. It is a current pattern, not a fixed identity."

## Product Surface Requirements

### Home

Home should show one clear result, not only progress.

Example:

- "Clear today: autonomy and quiet focus are leading signals."
- "Strongest current fit: self-directed work with room for depth."

### U-Map

U-Map should not only show axes. It should summarize what the axes mean.

Required summaries:

- strongest pattern
- weakest or least explored area
- best-fit condition
- likely friction condition
- next question target

### U-Map Report

U-Map Report should be the main surface for clear analysis results.

The normal U-Map can stay exploratory: axes, signals, records, movement, and
areas that are becoming clearer.

The U-Map Report should translate those clues into explicit results:

- what FI-YOU has learned clearly
- what the user likes and dislikes
- what the user repeatedly cares about
- what fits the user's current pattern
- what creates friction
- what kinds of work or role families match the pattern
- what kinds of teams, people, or relationship styles match the pattern
- what temperament and stress rhythm are repeatedly visible
- what is not yet supported by enough evidence

The U-Map Report is allowed to be more direct than ordinary clue cards.

Use:

- "Analysis result"
- "Clear result"
- "Strong fit"
- "Likely friction"
- "Repeated preference"
- "Current temperament pattern"
- "Recommended work-style family"

Avoid:

- "Maybe"
- "It seems"
- "A small clue"
- "This might be worth checking"

Required report sections:

1. Clear Results
2. Preference Results
3. Interest Results
4. Aptitude / Work Fit
5. Career Type Fit
6. Relationship Fit
7. Personality / Tendency / Temperament
8. Friction and Risk Conditions
9. Evidence
10. What Needs More Records

Each section should contain:

- one direct result sentence,
- evidence count or source labels,
- fit and friction where relevant,
- one next verification question.

Example:

```text
Clear result:
You fit better in environments where you can own decisions, protect deep focus,
and hold a high internal standard.

Evidence:
This appears across 11 records: decision style, values, Diary, and U-Map growth nodes.

Fit:
Research, strategy, writing, design craft, product thinking, or specialist roles
with clear ownership are stronger matches.

Friction:
Constant approval loops, vague authority, and frequent interruption are likely
to drain you faster.

Next verification:
Is your need for autonomy more about freedom, responsibility, privacy, or creative control?
```

U-Map Report should not say:

- "This is your fixed personality."
- "This is the one right career."
- "This person is good/bad for you."
- "FI-YOU knows your true self."

It should say:

- "This is the analysis result from your current records."
- "This is strong enough to present clearly."
- "This is not a permanent identity, but it is not a weak clue anymore."

### Insight Detail

Insight detail should include:

- assertion level: Observed / Repeated / Strong
- evidence count
- source types
- exact supporting records
- analysis result
- fit
- friction
- next check
- edit, hide, disagree, report

### Journy Report

Reports should be the most direct surface.

Each report should include:

- "What is clear"
- "What you like / dislike"
- "What you care about"
- "Where you fit"
- "Where friction appears"
- "People and environments that fit"
- "Temperament and stress rhythm"
- "What needs more evidence"

## Engine Requirements

The engine should not only generate "insights". It should generate structured self-knowledge claims.

Recommended output fields:

```json
{
  "claim": "Autonomy is one of your clearest current values.",
  "domain": "preference | interest | aptitude_fit | career_type_fit | relationship_fit | personality_tendency | temperament | stress_response",
  "certainty_level": "observed | repeated | strong",
  "evidence_count": 9,
  "source_types": ["question", "diary", "u_map"],
  "fit": ["self-directed work", "quiet focus", "clear ownership"],
  "friction": ["constant approval loops", "unclear authority", "frequent context switching"],
  "next_check": "Test whether autonomy is about freedom, responsibility, privacy, or creative control."
}
```

## Acceptance Criteria

FI-YOU passes this product standard when:

- A returning user can name at least three clear things FI-YOU has learned about them.
- Every strong claim shows evidence count and source records.
- Strong claims use direct language.
- Low-evidence areas are honestly marked as not enough data.
- The app distinguishes fit from destiny.
- The app distinguishes temperament from diagnosis.
- The app distinguishes relationship fit from judging another person.
- Users can correct, hide, or reject every claim.

## Final Rule

FI-YOU should be humble about the person and clear about the result.

Not:

> "You might be this kind of person."

But:

> "Analysis result: this is what your records repeatedly show. This is where you fit. This is where friction appears. This is what we should verify next."
